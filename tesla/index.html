What Full Self-Driving Actually Does

FSD lets the car handle the majority of driving tasks while Dad stays in the seat as a supervisor. It's designed to reduce the mental load and anxiety of driving — exactly what your family needs right now.

🛣️

Highway Autopilot — Austin to Houston & Dallas

On longer drives, the car handles steering, speed, lane changes, and following distance for the entire highway portion. Dad supervises from the driver's seat; the car does the work. This alone makes those trips far less exhausting.

🏙️

City Navigation — Errands & Church Runs

For daily Austin driving, FSD handles turns, traffic lights, stop signs, and navigating around other vehicles in urban traffic. It follows the navigation route autonomously.

Evening Confidence

Dad currently avoids driving at night. With FSD active, the car's cameras see clearly in all lighting conditions — giving him the confidence and capability to go out in the evening again.

Automatic Parking & Summon

The car parks itself in parallel or perpendicular spaces. Summon lets the car pull forward or backward out of tight spots on its own using the phone app.

📱

$99/Month Subscription — Flexible

FSD is now subscription-only — no large upfront cost. Subscribe when you want it, pause when you don't. Marcus can manage the subscription through the Tesla app remotely.

⚠️ Important to Understand

FSD is a Level 2 driver assistance system. Dad must remain attentive with hands available to take control at any time. It is not a fully autonomous chauffeur — Tesla calls it "Supervised" for this reason. Think of it as a highly capable co-pilot that handles most of the work, but the driver is always responsible. That said, it meaningfully reduces the stress and physical effort of driving.

On the Hardware Obsolescence Concern

The concern about Tesla hardware falling behind as FSD advances is valid — Tesla has already done this once, cycling from HW3 to HW4. But here's the key point for your situation:


At only 6,000 miles per year, a purchased Model Y will have roughly 30,000 miles after 5 years. That's nearly new by any standard. When — and if — hardware becomes a limitation, you'll be selling a low-mileage car that commands a strong price. That resale value effectively funds the upgrade to whatever Tesla offers then. Leasing protects against obsolescence but costs more over time. Buying at low mileage achieves the same goal through resale value, at lower total cost.

The Bottom Line

If the shorter time horizon is the most important factor — given Dad's age — then leasing is a reasonable hedge and there's no shame in that choice. But if the goal is best value and flexibility, buying wins. The math is clear once the Lexus NX proceeds are applied as a down payment.

Action Plan — In Order

1

Get the Lexus NX Value

Go to carmax.com or kbb.com for an instant offer on the 2018 Lexus NX. This tells you exactly how much you have for a down payment. CarMax offers are firm and good for 7 days — no obligation.

2

Apply for the Texas LDPLIP Grant

Apply to the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ) for the Light-Duty Plug-in Incentive Program — up to $2,500 back on a new EV purchase. This is first-come, first-served, so apply before purchasing.

3

Schedule a Test Drive (Recommended)

Have Mom and Dad experience the Model Y — and specifically the FSD — before committing. Tesla's Austin location offers free test drives. This is especially valuable so Dad can feel firsthand how FSD handles Austin traffic.

4

Configure & Order on Tesla.com

Go to tesla.com/modely and configure a Standard Range Model Y. Check current March 2026 delivery incentives — Tesla sometimes offers end-of-month discounts. You can order entirely online.

5

Arrange Level 2 Charger Installation

Get a quote from a licensed electrician (or through Tesla's installer referral). Budget ~$500–$1,500 installed. Apply for the Austin Energy 50% rebate (up to $1,200) and claim the federal $1,000 charger tax credit — available through June 30, 2026.

6

Set Up Tesla Account & FSD Subscription

Marcus sets up the Tesla account, links the smartphone app, and activates the $99/month FSD subscription. Walk Mom and Dad through the key controls — voice commands make most functions much easier. Plan at least one full practice session on local roads before any highway driving.

7

Sell the 2018 Lexus NX

Once the Tesla is delivered, sell the NX. Coordinate timing to minimize any gap. CarMax's instant offer is the simplest path — drive in, they write a check, done. Apply proceeds to pay down the Tesla loan immediately.

8

Plan the 2011 Lexus ES300 Transition

Keep Mom's ES300 for now. As she gets comfortable with the Tesla and FSD over the coming months, the ES300 naturally phases out. No rush — let the transition happen organically.
