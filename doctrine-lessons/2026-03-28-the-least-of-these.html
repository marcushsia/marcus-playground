Lesson Overview
Who Does Jesus See?

In Jesus' culture — and honestly, in ours — certain people were invisible: kids, foreigners, sick people, prisoners, the poor. Jesus didn't just notice them. He said His identity was wrapped up in them. Not just "help them." He says: when you help them, you're interacting with me.

Stories & Scripture
Matthew 19:14
"Let the little children come to me… for to such belongs the kingdom of heaven." Matthew 19:14 (ESV)

In Jesus' culture, kids had no status, no power, no voice. Adults literally shooed them away. Jesus stops, turns around, and centers them — and says the kingdom belongs to people like them.

→
The people others push aside are the ones Jesus pulls close.
Matthew 25:35 / Luke 10
"I was a stranger and you welcomed me…" Matthew 25:35 (ESV)

In the Parable of the Good Samaritan (Luke 10:33–35), the hero is the outsider — the Samaritan, someone Jews avoided. Jesus makes him the example of what love looks like.

→
Jesus calls us to welcome people who feel unfamiliar or uncomfortable — not just people like us.
Matthew 25:36
"I was sick and you visited me…" Matthew 25:36 (ESV)

Jesus touched lepers — people everyone else avoided completely. Not just healed them. Touched them. He didn't just fix their bodies; He restored their dignity.

→
Showing up for sick people — even when it's inconvenient — is showing up for Jesus.
Matthew 25:36
"…I was in prison and you came to me." Matthew 25:36 (ESV)

Society writes off prisoners — they made their choices, right? Jesus doesn't. He ties His own identity even to people behind bars.

→
Jesus doesn't write people off. He invites us to show up for the ones everyone else has forgotten.
Mark 12:43–44
"This poor widow has put in more than all of them." Mark 12:43–44 (ESV)

The disciples were impressed by the huge donations of the wealthy. Jesus pointed at the woman nobody noticed — two small coins — and said she gave the most. He consistently honors the poor and warns the rich.

→
Jesus flips our value system. The one with the least can give the most in His economy.
Luke 5:32
"I have not come to call the righteous but sinners to repentance." Luke 5:32 (ESV)

Jesus ate with tax collectors (traitors), talked with prostitutes, spent time with people religious leaders publicly avoided. He intentionally moved toward people with messy reputations.

→
The most surprising group Jesus moves toward? People everyone else avoids because of their sin.
Key Pattern
The common thread isn't just "widows and orphans." It's anyone who is overlooked, powerless, excluded, and can't repay you. Most of these groups can't advance your status and may even cost you socially. That's exactly why Jesus highlights them. And He doesn't just say "help them" — He says: when you do, you're interacting with Him.
Takeaways
Living It Out
•
Ask God to open your eyes to the people around you who are overlooked, lonely, or in need.
•
Choose one act of presence this week: show up for someone sick, share a meal, check in on someone forgotten.
•
Memorize Matthew 25:40 and let it shape how you see every interaction this week.
