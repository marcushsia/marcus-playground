Lesson Overview
More Than Forgiven

Salvation does not only mean God cancels our guilt. It means He brings us into His family. In adoption, God gives believers a new identity, a new Father, a new family, and a future inheritance. Christians do not live like spiritual orphans trying to earn a place at the table; they live as children who have been welcomed home.

Stories & Scripture
John 1:12 — Received as Children
"But to all who did receive him, who believed in his name, he gave the right to become children of God."John 1:12 (ESV)

Becoming God's child is not automatic because we are human, and it is not earned by being impressive. It is a gift given to those who receive Christ by faith.

→
Faith in Jesus gives us a family status we could never earn.
Romans 8:15–17 — No Longer Slaves
"The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God, and if children, then heirs..."Romans 8:16–17 (ESV)

Paul contrasts fear with adoption. A slave wonders if he is safe. A child is invited to come close. The Spirit teaches believers to cry "Father" instead of hiding from God.

→
Adoption replaces fear-driven religion with family closeness.
Luke 15 — The Lost Son
"This my son was dead, and is alive again; he was lost, and is found."Luke 15:24 (ESV)

The younger son returned with a speech about being treated like a servant. The father interrupted him with a robe, a ring, sandals, and a feast. That is adoption logic: not merely tolerated, but restored as a son.

→
God does not make repentant children live in the servant quarters.
Discussion
Where do you most often act like an orphan: trying to prove yourself, hiding when you fail, comparing yourself to others, or doubting that you are loved?
Takeaways
Living It Out
•
When you fail, run to the Father instead of hiding from Him.
•
Your identity starts with God's welcome, not your performance.
•
Treat other Christians as family, because God has adopted them too.
See what kind of love the Father has given to us, that we should be called children of God; and so we are.
