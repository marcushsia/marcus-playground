Create the Substack Account

Decide on a Go-Live Date

When does the first post go out on Substack? Once we have a date, the schedule is automatic — 2 posts per week, oldest post first, running all the way through 2029. We don't need to rush this; the posts will be ready whenever Pastor Pei wants to launch.

Marcus + Pastor Pei
4

Decide on Images

About 300 of the 347 post images were originally downloaded from the internet and may be copyrighted. We have two options:

Option A — Keep the originals. Low risk for a small newsletter, but technically the images aren't ours.

Option B — Generate new AI images. Using a tool called Nano Banana, we can create a unique, original image for each post based on its content. This takes more time but gives the newsletter a consistent, professional, fully original look.

Either way, the 14 original photos taken by Pastor Pei or family will be kept as-is.

Marcus + Pastor Pei

🗓 What's Coming Next

Phase 4 — Format Posts for Substack
Each post will be cleaned up and formatted properly for Substack. Images will be attached. Any broken links will be fixed or removed based on the decisions above. A sample of 10 posts will be reviewed before bulk upload.
Phase 5 — Upload & Schedule
All 347 posts will be loaded into Substack and scheduled at 2 posts per week in chronological order. That's about 3.5 years of content ready to go out automatically.
Phase 6 — Handoff to Pastor Pei
Once everything is live, Marcus will walk Pastor Pei through how to use Substack — how to see subscribers, respond to comments, and eventually write new posts directly on the platform.
💡 The big picture: Once set up, Substack will automatically send Pastor Pei's writing to subscribers by email twice a week — with no extra work required. People can subscribe for free, share posts easily, and new readers can find the blog through Substack's own discovery tools.
